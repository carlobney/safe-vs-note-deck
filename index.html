SAFE vs
Convertible Note

What every founder should know before signing

What Are They?

🟢 SAFE

Simple Agreement for Future Equity

  • Created by Y Combinator
  • Converts into equity at a future round
  • Not a debt instrument

🔴 Convertible Note

Short-term debt → future equity

  • Traditional instrument
  • Converts into equity at a future round
  • Is a loan

The Most Important Difference

Debt vs. Not Debt

SAFE

  • Not a loan
  • No repayment obligation
  • No maturity date
  • No interest

Convertible Note

  • Is a loan
  • Has principal
  • Accrues interest (4–8%)
  • Has a maturity date

Maturity Date

SAFE

  • No maturity date
  • Stays outstanding until a triggering event
  • Priced round, acquisition, etc.

Convertible Note

  • 12–24 month deadline
  • If no round → investors can extend, convert, or...
  • demand repayment

Interest

SAFE

0%

No interest. Ever.

Convertible Note

4–8%

Accrues over time, converts to equity along with principal.

Complexity

SAFE

  • Simpler document
  • Faster to close
  • Fewer negotiation points
  • Founder-friendly

Convertible Note

  • More complex
  • Negotiates: interest rate, maturity, cap, discount
  • Default provisions
  • More attorney time

Investor Protection

SAFE — Founder Wins

  • No repayment rights
  • No time pressure
  • Cleaner cap table

Note — Investor Wins

  • Legal debt claim
  • Maturity creates urgency
  • More leverage at conversion

When to Use Each

Use a SAFE when…

  • Pre-seed or seed stage
  • Small angel checks
  • You need to close fast
  • Founder-leaning ecosystem (e.g. Silicon Valley)

Use a Note when…

  • Investors want downside protection
  • Concern about timeline to next round
  • Larger angel checks
  • Investors insist

TL;DR

SAFE

Simple. Fast. No debt. Founder-friendly. The default for early-stage in today's market.

Convertible Note

More investor protection. More complexity. Use it when investors ask for it — or when you have real leverage and they don't.

When in doubt → use a SAFE.
